Recipes

Tiramisu

Prep Time
30 min
Difficulty
Medium
Servings
6 servings

Description

The name itself gives us a clue as to its origin. It comes from the Italian Tiramisù, which literally means “pull me up”, but we could translate it as “raise me up”, since this delicious Italian dessert takes us to seventh heaven. It owes its name to the fact that the ingredients needed to make it are very energetic: eggs, sugar, coffee and mascarpone cheese.

Tiramisù was created at the end of the 16th century to pay tribute to the Duke of Tuscany, Cosimo III de Medici, upon his arrival in Siena, so that he would have fond memories of the city. For the occasion, Sienese pastry chefs prepared a dessert using the finest ingredients, namely mascarpone cheese, chocolate and coffee.

Ingredients

For the creamy mixture:

  • 1 1/2 cups heavy whipping cream
  • 8 ounce container mascarpone cheese , room temperature
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For layering:

  • 1 package Lady Fingers
  • 1 1/2 cups cold espresso
  • 3 Tablespoons of liqueur
  • Cocoa powder

Method

  1. Add whipping creamto a mixing bowl and beat on medium speed with electric mixers (or use a stand mixer). Slowly add sugar and vanilla and continue to beat until stiff peaks. Add mascarpone cheese and fold in until combined. Set aside.
  2. Add t coffee and liqueur to a shallow bowl. Dip the lady fingers in the coffee (Don't soak them--just quickly dip them on both sides to get them wet) and lay them in a single layer on the bottom of an 8x8'' or similar size pan.
  3. Smooth half of the mascarpone mixture over the top. Add another layer of dipped lady fingers. Smooth remaining mascarpone cream over the top.
  4. Dust cocoa powder generously over the top (I use a fine mesh strainer to do this). Refrigerate for at least 3-4 hours or up to overnight before serving.
